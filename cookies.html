Cookie Policy

Last updated: 2 July 2026

1. What cookies are

Cookies are small text files a website stores on your device to remember information between visits. Some are essential to how a site works; others are optional and help site owners understand usage. We also use your browser's local storage (a similar but separate technology) for one specific purpose, described below.

2. The choice you get

When you first visit, a banner lets you Accept All, Reject Non-Essential, or open Manage Preferences to choose analytics on or off individually. You can change your mind at any time using the Cookie Settings link in the footer of every page.

We do not use advertising, retargeting, or cross-site tracking cookies of any kind.

4. Product usage events (only if you've accepted analytics)

When analytics cookies are accepted, our tools send a small number of anonymous usage events alongside the cookies above — currently:

EventWhat it containsWhy
validation_runPass/fail result, and a count of errors and warnings. No project content.Tells us whether the validation feature is catching real issues.
mod_exportedCounts of systems, empires, origins and events in the exported mod. No names, no custom text, no project content.Tells us the tool is actually being used to ship mods, and roughly how big they are.

These events never include anything you've typed or named — no system, empire, or event content is ever sent. If you reject analytics cookies, none of this fires.

5. Local storage used by our tools

The Stellaris Mod Builder and our other in-browser tools are not cookies in the technical sense, but they do use your browser's local storage to save your project data (systems, empires, settings, UI preferences) directly on your device. We disclose it here for transparency: this storage is essential to the tool working at all, is never transmitted anywhere, and is entirely under your control — clearing your browser's site data removes it permanently, including from us, since we never had a copy.

6. Managing cookies in your browser

Beyond our own banner, you can block or delete cookies through your browser's settings at any time, and you can opt out of Google Analytics specifically using Google's browser add-on. Blocking necessary cookies may affect how the site remembers your preferences.

7. Changes to this policy

We'll update this page if the cookies we use change. See our Privacy Policy for how we handle personal data more broadly.
