Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2 July 2026

1. Who we are

Proelium Artifex ("we", "us", "our") is a two-practice studio: a software consultancy (project delivery, solution design, consultancy, AI & automation) and a gaming practice (modding, tools, and world-building for games such as Stellaris). This policy covers proeliumartifex.com and the free tools we publish there, including the Stellaris Mod Builder.

Contact: hello@proeliumartifex.com.

2. Information we collect

Information you give us directly

If you email us, message us through GitHub, Discord, Nexus Mods, YouTube, Patreon or Ko-fi, or otherwise contact us, we receive whatever you include in that message — typically your name, email or handle, and message content. Those platforms are operated by third parties and are also subject to their own privacy policies.

Information collected automatically (only with your consent)

We do not run analytics or tracking of any kind unless you accept analytics cookies via the consent banner. If you accept, we use Google Analytics to understand aggregate usage of the site — pages visited, approximate location (city/country level), device and browser type, and referring site. We do not use advertising cookies, and we do not sell or share data for advertising purposes. See our Cookie Policy for the full list of cookies and what each one does.

Information stored only in your browser

The Stellaris Mod Builder and our other in-browser tools save your work — systems, empires, projects, and settings — using your browser's local storage (localStorage). This data never leaves your device: it is not transmitted to us, not uploaded to any server, and we have no access to it, no copy of it, and no way to recover it if you clear your browser data. Exporting or importing a project file is something only you initiate, and those files pass through your own device.

3. How we use information

  • To operate, maintain and improve the site and our tools.
  • To respond to enquiries sent to us directly.
  • Where you've consented, to understand how the site is used in aggregate, so we can improve it.

4. Legal bases for processing (EEA/UK visitors)

Where GDPR applies, we rely on: consent for analytics cookies (which you can withdraw at any time via "Cookie Settings" in the footer); legitimate interests for responding to enquiries and keeping the site secure and functioning; and contract where we're discussing or delivering consultancy work with you.

5. Sharing your information

We don't sell your information. We share it only with: Google, as our analytics processor, if and only if you've accepted analytics cookies; and the third-party platforms you choose to interact with us through (GitHub, Nexus Mods, YouTube, Patreon, Ko-fi), which operate under their own privacy policies.

6. Data retention

Your consent cookie is kept for up to 180 days, after which you'll be asked again. Analytics data, when enabled, is retained according to Google Analytics' default settings (currently up to 14 months) unless we configure otherwise. Correspondence is kept as long as reasonably necessary to handle your enquiry and for our records.

7. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to access, correct, delete, or export your personal data, to object to or restrict our processing, and to withdraw consent at any time. To exercise any of these, email hello@proeliumartifex.com. You can change your cookie consent at any time using the "Cookie Settings" link in the site footer.

8. Children

Our site and tools are not directed at children, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

9. International transfers

Where analytics is enabled, Google may process data on servers outside your country of residence, under its own compliance mechanisms.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the most recent revision.

11. A note on how this policy was prepared

This policy is a plain-English template intended to accurately describe our current data practices. It is not a substitute for legal advice, and we recommend it (and the governing-law details in our Terms of Use) be reviewed by a qualified professional for your specific jurisdiction before you rely on it for formal compliance purposes.
